Reforming, Not Reformed

This isn't a redemption arc. It's a work in progress, and I'm done pretending otherwise.

RB
Rama Ben Khalifa

Everyone wants the after-photo. The clean apartment, the green juice, the caption about 'healing.' Nobody wants the part where you're still figuring it out at 1am with your phone face-down on the table because you don't trust yourself with it yet.

I'm Rama Ben Khalifa, and I want to be very clear about something before you read anything else on this site: I have not arrived anywhere. I am reforming, present continuous, not reformed, past tense. Those are different words for a reason.

A redemption arc has an ending. Mine doesn't come with one, and I stopped waiting for the credits to roll.

For a long time the story other people told about me had a shape: party girl, cautionary tale, mess. It was easier for them than the truth, which is that I am a Gemini sun who can hold six versions of myself at once, a Scorpio moon who feels everything like a verdict, and a person who has spent years testing exactly how far 'off the rails' could go before something broke for good.

Something did break, more than once. I'm not going to smooth that over here just to make myself easier to root for.

What I can tell you is that reforming, for me, doesn't look like willpower. It looks like writing this down in public, where I can't quietly take it back tomorrow. It looks like naming the pattern instead of just living inside it. This magazine is that — not a highlight reel, not a warning label. Just the log, story by story, of a person trying to become someone she can trust.
