What My Friends Won't Say To My Face

The group chat I wasn't in, the interventions that weren't, and the friendships that didn't survive the old me.

RB
Rama Ben Khalifa

I found out about the group chat by accident, the way you find out about most things people are protecting you from. It wasn't cruel. It was worse than cruel — it was careful, the kind of careful people get when they've quietly downgraded you from 'friend having a rough patch' to 'friend we're managing.'

Nobody staged an intervention. Nobody sat me down. Instead, invitations got quieter. Check-ins got shorter. People started saying 'let's do something low-key' in a tone that meant something specific about me they weren't going to say out loud.

The scariest thing isn't being called out. It's realizing people cared enough to talk about you and not enough, anymore, to talk to you.

Some of those friendships I've since repaired, slowly, by being a person worth trusting again instead of asking to be trusted on credit. Some of them are just gone, and I've had to make peace with the fact that I don't get to relitigate that with people who already made their decision while I wasn't looking.

I'm writing this one for anyone who's ever found their own 'concerned' group chat. It doesn't feel like love in the moment. Sometimes it is anyway.
