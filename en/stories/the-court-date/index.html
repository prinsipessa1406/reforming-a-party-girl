The Court Date

What it's like to sit in a room and answer for the version of you that stopped listening.

RB
Rama Ben Khalifa

There's a particular kind of quiet in a room where a stranger in a robe is about to say your full name out loud. Rama Ben Khalifa. Not the nickname my friends use, not the handle on my old party photos — the whole legal weight of it.

I'm not going to use this space to relitigate the specifics of what got me there. That's not the point of the story, and honestly it's not anyone's business but mine and the people directly involved. What I will say is that consequences had been circling for a while, and I had gotten very good at outrunning them, until the day I couldn't.

I used to think getting away with something meant I'd won. That day I learned the difference between getting away with it and getting away from it.

Sitting there, I kept doing this thing where I tried to perform remorse correctly, like there was a rubric. Eventually I stopped performing and just felt it, which was worse and also, weirdly, the first honest thing I'd done in months.

I left that building different than I walked in. Not fixed — I want to resist that word every time it tries to sneak into this site — but accountable in a way that finally stuck to my skin instead of sliding off. Some lessons you can only learn from someone with the actual authority to make you sit still long enough to hear them.
