Scorpio Moon, Broken Rules

What my chart says about why I always went too far — and why I don't blame the stars anymore.

RB
Rama Ben Khalifa

Gemini sun, Scorpio moon, Scorpio rising. I've used this chart as an explanation, an excuse, and eventually just a description, in roughly that order.

The Gemini sun wants novelty, wants six conversations at once, gets bored of a room before it's finished being interesting. The Scorpio moon and rising want intensity or nothing — no polite middle setting, no 'a little bit' of a feeling. Put those in one body and you get someone who doesn't just go to the party, she needs the party to mean something, to be the most anything that has ever happened.

For years I said 'it's a Scorpio moon thing' the way other people say 'it's just how I'm wired,' as if that ended the conversation instead of starting it.

Here's what I've come around to: the chart explains the shape of the hunger, not the choices I made to feed it. A Scorpio moon doesn't write a check with your name on it. A Scorpio moon doesn't sit in a courtroom for you either.

I still love my chart. I still think it's a genuinely useful map of why I feel like the volume knob is missing on my own emotions. But I've stopped handing it the steering wheel. These days it's context, not a defense.
