Rock Bottom Had Wifi

On the night everything caught up with me, and why I'm grateful it did.

RB
Rama Ben Khalifa

Rock bottom, for me, did not look cinematic. There was no rain, no mascara running down my face in slow motion. There was a hallway floor, a phone at 4% battery, and the specific, humiliating clarity of realizing I could not remember the last two hours of my own life.

I want to write around this story. I've written around it for a long time, told the version with better lighting to people at dinner. Here's the plainer one: I had been telling myself 'one more night' for months, and that night the bill came due in a way I couldn't negotiate with.

The scariest part wasn't the blackout. It was how unbothered I was by it the next morning, until I wasn't.

There's a strange grief in outgrowing a version of yourself you also loved, a little. That girl was fun. People wanted her at the party. She got invited everywhere and belonged nowhere, including inside her own head.

I'm not telling this story for sympathy and I'm not telling it as a morality tale either. I'm telling it because pretending it didn't happen is exactly the kind of quiet dishonesty that let it happen fifty times before. Reforming a party girl starts with stopping the edit.
